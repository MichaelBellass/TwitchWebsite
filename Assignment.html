1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites.


When creating a website it is a general rule that anything you wish to put on there should be assumed to be protected. If any content provided on your website doesn’t belong to you it is best to reach to the original owner of the content to avoid unauthorised content and law suits. Content such as images used on websites that can be found on other 3rd party websites can easily be mistaken to be “royal” or “copyright” free. This is when you would need to read the click-wrap agreement which is often overlooked to specify intended uses. If you should be in a situation where a complaint has been made against you on unauthorised materials on your website, first and foremost it is best to remove this material. Even if you feel it appropriate, its best to consult with a lawyer to discuss fair use of the material. Unauthorised use of material can interrupt workflow and completion of any website projects causing failure to meet specific deadlines. Unless you are using material that you can for certain claim as your own, it is always best to do the research and seek out the original owner’s permission before making your website public. The internet is constantly updating, and businesses are very protective of content they make. It is much easier these days for website creators to be caught using someone else’s content. So, when in doubt always seek permission.

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them


A website is going to be accessed by all so it’s important to make any necessary alterations for those with a disability. This includes
- Use of Font sizes
A clear and well structured website with a large readable font for those who are visually impaired.
- Colour Scheme
Making correct use of colours, ensuring backgrounds and font colours are not too bright or harsh and make it difficult for the user to read.
- Using subtitles in videos
Websites such as YouTube will heavily implement this to appeal to all audiences. The use of a transcript in videos is a helpful resource for users.
- Appropriate use of periods in abbreviations
For those who are using “screen read” on their systems, appropriate language and use of abbreviations can help the user to transcribe what is being read.
- A simple and cohesive website.
Keeping a simple layout for a website will help to keep it more organised in general. Using correct spacing between content, accessible descriptive links and clear texts are helpful.
